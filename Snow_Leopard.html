Snow Leopard - The Snow Panther



About the Snow Leopard

Snow leopard is a cat species of big cats in the Panthera family, which is native to Asia. It looks like a leopard but is different from a normal leopard. Snow leopard is smaller than a leopard and cannot roar or growl. But the thing that surprises everyone is, it is related to tigers. Considering its fur, there are spots on the greyish-white coat, and it is thick. This fur keeps these creatures well insulated in cold weather. Its fur can be 5 cm long on their sides and back and almost 12 cm long on their belly. A snow leopard's body is 90 - 210 cm in length.

  • Scientific name: Panthera uncia
  • Mass: 22 - 55 kg (Male), 34 - 48 kg (Female)
  • Height: 60 cm (Adult, At shoulder)
  • Speed: 64 - 80 km/h
  • Bite force: 500 PSI
  • Lifespan: 10 - 12 years (in the wild)
  • Eats: Ibex, marmots, deer, blue sheep, pikas, argali wild sheep, goats
  • Conservation status: Vulnerable

How do Snow Leopards roar?

Actually, snow leopards can't roar. They only can meow, yowl, growl, and purr. Their purring is cute as domestic cats.

Subspecies of Snow Leopard

There are three subspecies of snow leopard in the world. They are:

  1. Panthera uncia uncia
  2. Panthera uncia uncioides
  3. Panthera uncia irbis

Popular National Parks and Snow Leopard Reserves

Popular National Parks and Snow Leopard Reserves in Asia

Snow leopards are primarily found in the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. Here are some of the key locations:

  • Hemis National Park (India)

    This park is home to around 200 snow leopards.

    Hemis National Park, located in the Indian state of Ladakh, is known for its stunning high-altitude landscapes and abundant wildlife, including the elusive snow leopard, Himalayan ibex, and Tibetan wolf.

    Find more
  • Sagarmatha National Park (Nepal)

    The park is home to a small population of snow leopards.

    Sagarmatha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, encompasses the Mount Everest region and is renowned for its rugged terrain, Sherpa culture, and diverse wildlife, including the elusive snow leopard.

    Find more
  • Altai Tavan Bogd National Park (Mongolia)

    There are an estimated 30-40 snow leopards in the park.

    Altai Tavan Bogd National Park is located in western Mongolia and is characterized by its spectacular mountain landscapes, ancient petroglyphs, and a variety of rare wildlife, including snow leopards and argali sheep.

    Find more
  • Sarychat-Ertash State Reserve (Kyrgyzstan)

    The reserve is known for its significant population of snow leopards.

    Sarychat-Ertash State Reserve, located in the Tien Shan Mountains of Kyrgyzstan, provides critical habitat for snow leopards and other endangered species, offering stunning scenery of glacial valleys and high peaks.

    Find more
Other Notable Snow Leopard Habitats
  • Tost Mountains (Mongolia)

    The Tost Mountains are recognized as a community-protected area for snow leopards.

    The Tost Mountains are located in the South Gobi region of Mongolia and are a critical habitat for snow leopards, thanks to local conservation efforts and community engagement.

    Find more
  • Karakoram Range (Pakistan)

    Snow leopards are found in various protected areas within this mountain range.

    The Karakoram Range, straddling the borders of Pakistan, India, and China, is famous for its towering peaks, including K2, and serves as an important habitat for snow leopards.
