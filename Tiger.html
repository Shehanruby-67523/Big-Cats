Tiger - The Orange Menace



About the Tiger

Tiger is the largest and strongest big cat in the world. This big cat is native to Asia. It has a muscular, powerful body with 2.9 m length, large paws, a big head, orange fur with black, mostly vertical stripes, and a long tail. Tigers are apex predators in their regions. The tiger also has the roughest tongue in the animal world.

  • Scientific name: Panthera tigris
  • Mass: 90 - 310kg (Male, Adult), 65 - 170kg (Female, Adult)
  • Height: 0.90 - 1.10m (Adult, At shoulder)
  • Speed: 49 - 65 km/h
  • Bite force: 1050 PSI
  • Lifespan: 10 - 15 years
  • Eats: Sambar deer, Wild boar
  • Conservation status: Endangered

Subspecies of Tigers

There are overall nine tiger subspecies but three of them are already extinct now. It means there are only six subspecies left in the world.

The remaining subspecies are:

  1. Bengal tiger (Panthera tigris tigris)
  2. Siberian tiger (Panthera tigris altaica)
  3. Sumatran tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae)
  4. Indochinese tiger (Panthera tigris corbetti)
  5. Malayan tiger (Panthera tigris jacksoni)
  6. South China tiger (Panthera tigris amoyensis)

The following subspecies are extinct. They are:

  1. Caspian tiger (Panthera tigris virgata)
  2. Bali tiger (Panthera tigris balica)
  3. Javan tiger (Panthera tigris sondaica)

Amomg these subspecies, Siberian tiger is the largest tiger species in the world.
