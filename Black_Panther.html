About the Black Panther

Black panther is not a particular big cat species. They are actually leopards and jaguars. A black panther is the melanistic colour variant of the leopard and the jaguar. Black panthers of both species have excess black pigments, but their typical rosettes are also present. They have been documented mostly in tropical forests, with black leopards in Africa and Asia, and black jaguars in South America.

  • Height: 60 - 70 cm (Adult, At shoulder)

Popular National Parks and Black Panther Reserves

Popular National Parks and Black Panther Reserves in Africa and Asia

Black panthers, which are melanistic leopards or jaguars, can be found in various regions across Africa and Asia. Here are some key locations:

  • Kabini Wildlife Sanctuary (India)

    The sanctuary is famous for sightings of black panthers.

    Kabini Wildlife Sanctuary, located in Karnataka, India, is renowned for its diverse wildlife, including tigers, leopards, and the elusive black panther, which is actually a melanistic leopard.

  • "Sripada" Peak Wilderness Sanctuary (Sri Lanka)

    Black panthers are a rare sight here, but they have been spotted occasionally.

    In 2022, a black leopard was found in Delhousie (Nallathanniya), which is near to this wilderness sanctuary.

    Find more
  • Malanad Forests (India)

    The Malanad region is one of the few places where black panthers have been spotted in India.

    The Malanad forests, located in the Western Ghats of India, are rich in biodiversity and home to a variety of wildlife, including the rare black panther.

    Find more
  • Chandrapur Forest (India)

    There have been sightings of black panthers in this forest reserve.

    Chandrapur Forest, located in Maharashtra, India, is known for its wildlife, including tigers, leopards, and rare sightings of black panthers.

    Find more
  • Cuyo Game Reserve (South Africa)

    Occasionally, black leopards have been spotted here.

    Cuyo Game Reserve is known for its efforts in leopard conservation, and there have been rare sightings of black leopards in this area.
