About the Leopard

Leopard is a cat species from the genus Panthera. The name "leopard" comes from the Latin word "leopardus". Leopardus is a compound of 'leo', which means 'lion', and 'pardus' means 'spotted'. That word represents the meaning "Spotted lion". Its fur is yellowish to dark golden with dark spots grouped in rosettes. The leopard has a muscular and slender body with a length of 92 - 183 cm.

  • Scientific name: Panthera pardus
  • Mass: 30.9 - 72 kg (Male), 20.5 - 43 kg (Female)
  • Height: 60 - 70 cm (Adult male, At shoulder), 57 - 64 cm (Adult female, At shoulder)
  • Speed: 58 km/h
  • Bite force: 300 PSI
  • Lifespan: 12 - 17 years (in the wild)
  • Eats: Fish, reptiles, baboons, hares, strong-scented carrion, antelopes, wildbeasts, and warthogs
  • Conservation status: Vulnerable

Subspecies of Leopard

There are nine subspecies of leopard in the world. They are:

  1. African leopard (Panthera pardus pardus)
  2. Indian leopard (Panthera pardus fusca)
  3. Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya)
  4. Amur leopard (Panthera pardus orientalis)
  5. Persian leopard (Panthera pardus tulliana)
  6. North China leopard (Panthera pardus japonensis)
  7. Javan leopard (Panthera pardus melas)
  8. Indochinese leopard (Panthera pardus delacouri)
  9. Arabian leopard (Panthera pardus nimr)

Amomg these subspecies, Persian leopard is the largest species in the world while the Arabian leopard is the smallest subspecies.
