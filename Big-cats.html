About Big Cats

Simply, big cats are the large members of the cat family. They live in diverse habitats like tropical rainforests, jungles, savannahs, snow forests, and deserts. We can find them in every region except the Arctic and Antarctic. All big cats are carnivores, and many are apex predators in their regions, meaning they sit at the top of the food chain.

The term "big cat" typically refers to seven magnificent creatures: the five members of the Panthera genus - tiger, lion, jaguar, leopard, and snow leopard - in addition, the non-pantherine cougar and the speedy cheetah.
