Jaguar - The Jungle King



About the Jaguar

Jaguar is a big cat species and the only member of the genus Panthera, which is native to the American regions. It is the member of big cat, which has the strongest bite force among their members. With a weight of up to 158 kg and a body length of up to 1.85 m, Jaguar is the largest cat species in North and South America and the third largest big cat in the world.

  • Scientific name: Panthera onca
  • Mass: 56 - 96 kg (Adult)
  • Height: 69-79 cm (Adult, At shoulder)
  • Speed: 80 km/h
  • Bite force: 1500 PSI
  • Lifespan: 12 - 15 years (in the wild)
  • Eats: peccaries, capybaras, pacas, agoutis, deer, opossum, rabbits, armadillos, caimans, turtles, livestock, anacondas, birds and fish species.
  • Conservation status: Near Threatened

Lower Classification of Jaguar

There are nine jaguar subspecies in the world.

  1. Arizona jaguar (Panthera onca arizonensis)
  2. Goldman's jaguar (Panthera onca goldmani)
  3. South American jaguar (Panthera onca onca)
  4. Paraguay jaguar (Panthera onca paraguensis)
  5. Pantanal jaguar (Panthera onca palustris)
  6. West Mexican jaguar (Panthera onca hernandesii)
  7. Vera Cruz jaguar (Panthera onca veraecrucis)
  8. Peruvian jaguar (Panthera onca peruviana)
  9. Central American jaguar (Panthera onca centralis)

Among these species, Pantanal jaguar is the largest jaguar subspecies.

Popular National Parks and Jaguar Reserves

Popular National Parks and Jaguar Reserves in North America

In North America, We can find Jaguars from:

  • Corcovado National Park, Costa Rica

    There are approximately 50 jaguars in this national park.

    Corcovado National Park is a renowned protected area in Costa Rica, known for its diverse ecosystems and abundant wildlife. Corcovado is home to a variety of mammals, including jaguars, tapirs, sloths, and monkeys. It's also a birder's paradise, with over 400 species of birds recorded.

    Find more
  • Calakmul Biosphere Reserve, Mexico

    There are approximately 500 jaguars in this national park.

    Calakmul Biosphere Reserve is a vast protected area in southern Mexico, known for its dense tropical rainforest and ancient Mayan ruins. The reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including jaguars, tapirs, monkeys, and a wide range of bird species.

    Find more
  • Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary, Belize

    There are approximately 200 jaguars in this national park.

    Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area in Belize, known for its diverse wildlife and stunning natural beauty. It's the world's first jaguar reserve and is home to a significant population of these elusive big cats.

    Find more
Popular National Parks and Jaguar Reserves in South America

In South America, we can find jaguars from:

  • Iwokrama Forest, Guyana

    There are nearly 1000 jaguars in this place.

    Find more
  • Manú National Park, Peru

    Manu is famous for its rich biodiversity. You can spot a variety of mammals like jaguars, monkeys, tapirs, and sloths. Birdwatchers will be delighted by the abundance of species, including the harpy eagle, macaws, and toucans.

    Find more
  • Tambopata National Reserve, Peru

    The Tambopata National Reserve is a protected area located in southeastern Peru, in the Amazon rainforest. It is known for its diverse wildlife, including jaguars, monkeys, birds, and reptiles. The reserve is also home to the Tambopata River, which is a popular destination for kayaking and fishing.

    Find more
  • Cuiabá River, northern Pantanal, Brazil

    The Pantanal is known for its diverse wildlife, including a significant population of jaguars. The Cuiabá River, as a major waterway in the region, is likely to be within the range of these big cats.

    Find more

South America has the largest population of jaguars in the world.
