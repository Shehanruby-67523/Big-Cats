Cougar - The Ghost Cat



About the Cougar

The cougar is a non-Pantherine cat species among big cats. It is more related to a domestic cat. Puma, mountain lion, catamount, and panther are similar names for cougar. This cat species is native to the Americas, in North, Central, and South America. The cougar is the terrestrial mammal, which is the most widely distributed in the Western Hemisphere. It has short, coarse hair that is rust, light brown, lemon, apricot, black, or smoke. Cougars grow up to 7 to 8 feets long.

  • Scientific name: Puma concolor
  • Mass: 53 - 72 kg (Male), 34 - 48 kg (Female)
  • Height: 53 - 71 cm (Adult, At shoulder)
  • Speed: 64 - 80 km/h
  • Bite force: 724.6 PSI
  • Lifespan: 8 - 13 years (in the wild)
  • Eats: deer, elk, moose, mountain goats, wild sheep, chickens, raccoons, rabbits, and coyotes
  • Conservation status: Least Concern (Population decreasing)

How do Cougars roar?

The reality is cougars can't roar. They can only purr, growl and scream. Cougar is the largest cat who can meow and purr. Their sound is kind of cute as domestic cats.

Subspecies of Cougar

There have been some descriptions of 32 cougar subspecies. There are six subspecies of cougar, according to genetic analysis. They are:

  1. Costa Rican cougar (Puma concolor costaricensis)
  2. Florida Panther / North American cougar (Puma concolor couguar)
  3. Northen South American cougar (Puma concolor concolor)
  4. Eastern South American cougar (Puma concolor anthonyi)
  5. Southern South American cougar (Puma concolor puma)
  6. Argentine puma / Central South American cougar (Puma concolor cabrerae)

The current valid subspecies are:

  • South American cougar (Puma concolor concolor)
  • North and Central American cougar (Puma concolor couguar)

Amomg these subspecies, North American cougar is the largest cougar species in the world.
